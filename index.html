I am a PhD student in Informatics at the University of Edinburgh, working on networks and systems for AI — defect-aware network-on-chip (NoC) architectures, network simulation for distributed AI, and system software for wafer-scale accelerators. Before my PhD I spent several years in the semiconductor industry at Cadence and TSMC, working on advanced process nodes, EDA tooling, and computational lithography.

Projects

Defect-Tolerant Wafer-Scale Routing (DLR)

Defect-aware routing for wafer-scale interconnects, evaluated on two purpose-built NoC simulators in Rust — an event-driven cycle-accurate model (VC flow control, custom arbitration & switch allocation) and a flow-based analytical model for design-space exploration.

RustNoCWafer-Scale

SysSim — Communication Cost Model

Contributed a Gaussian-Process-based communication cost model to SysSim, an open-source distributed-AI simulator, reducing latency-prediction error versus the prior linear baseline.

PythonGaussian ProcessML Systems

LLM Kernel-Offloading Models

Performance models for automatic kernel offloading in large-language-model training, guiding when offloading compute improves overall throughput.
